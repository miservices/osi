Protecting the Sovereignty, Security, and Interests of the State of Michigan

The Office of State Intelligence (OSI) is a principal office within the executive branch of Michigan, responsible for the collection, analysis, and dissemination of intelligence relating to threats to this state — including foreign intelligence, counterintelligence, cyber threats, terrorism, and espionage.

Core Responsibilities

Foreign Intelligence

Monitoring and assessing foreign activities impacting Michigan’s economic, technological, and security interests.

Counterintelligence

Identifying and disrupting espionage, infiltration, and unlawful foreign influence within the state.

Cyber Defense

Detecting and mitigating cyber threats targeting state infrastructure, public systems, and industry.

Intelligence Assessments

Providing intelligence briefings to the Governor and authorized state officials.
